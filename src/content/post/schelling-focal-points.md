*The most powerful rules in your workplace are the ones that were never written.*

Imagine your phone dies in a crowded Bengaluru mall. You were supposed to meet a friend, but you never fixed a spot. No WhatsApp, no call. Where do you go? Chances are, you both head to the main entrance. Not because someone told you to. Not because it's written anywhere. But because it's *obvious* — the kind of obvious that doesn't need explaining.

That instinct has a name. It's called a **Schelling focal point**.

## Thomas Schelling and the Problem of Pure Coordination

The idea comes from Thomas Schelling, who studied how people coordinate without communication. In "pure coordination" situations, we don't compete — we just try to guess what others will do. And surprisingly, we often converge on the same choice, guided by what feels obvious or natural.

That's exactly how workplaces function beneath the surface. Many norms — when to speak up, how fast to reply, what's "acceptable" — aren't written anywhere. Yet teams quickly align around them because they become shared focal points.
